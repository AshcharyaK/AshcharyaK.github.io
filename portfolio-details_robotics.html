Robotics

  • Category: MATLAB and Robotic Manipulation
  • Project date: February-March 2023

Description

This project was conducted in groups of 3 where the main task was to be able to control and perform tasks and DYNAMAXIEL Robot arm. There were four main sections to this project:

  • Section A: Modelling the Robot Arm by choosing an appropriate coordinate frame to develop the DH table. Moreover, computing and thereafter, modelling the forward kinematics on MATLAB simulation which determines the end-effector position based on the specified joint angles. Finally, developing and graphically modelling the Inverse Kinematics equation from the DH table by simplifying the robotic arm into 2 segments. This helps to compute each joint angle required to reach the specified end-effector position.

  • Section B: The aim of this task was to pick and place a cube from a specified initial position to an end-position (i.e. translation). Moreover, a sub-task included to re-orient the cube such that the red side faced towards the audience from a known initial orientation of the cube block. This involved inverse kinematics but also, incorporating an appropriate path the robotic arm follows whilst translating the block between certain positions to avoid interfering with surrounding objects in addition to moving quickly and efficiently. Cubic interpolation was utilised for translation as it reflects industry practices, particularly with large robots where inertia combined with fast speeds become more prominent.

  • Section C: This tested the accuracy of the robotic arm as it had to draw a specified shape that involved gripping a pen automatically and then drawing a triangle with a semi-circle. It was important to interpolate the start and end positions to ensure an accurate semi-circle.

  • Section D: This section provided the creativity to perform any task of our choice. Our choice involved the audience teaching the robot the tasks to be performed to which the robot utilised inverse kinematics continuously to calculate the appropriate joint angles.

    • Finally, I learnt SolidWorks to produce and 3D print a gripper for section C.
